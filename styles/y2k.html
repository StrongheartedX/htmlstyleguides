Y2K

Design System

Chrome surfaces, bubble interfaces, and iridescent optimism from the digital frontier of 2000.

The dawn of a new millennium brought boundless optimism about technology. Shiny interfaces, translucent gadgets, and chrome-plated everything promised a future where digital life would be sleek, fun, and endlessly connected. 05

Forms

Frosted-glass inputs with rounded pill shapes and glowing focus states. Form elements feel touchable and dimensional, like translucent plastic controls.

06

Cards & Panels

Three card styles capture different facets of Y2K: chrome metallic surfaces, translucent bubble glass, and shimmering iridescent borders.

📱

Mobile Revolution

Flip phones with chrome bezels and translucent cases. Snake on every screen. The future fits in your pocket.

💻

Dot-Com Dreams

Every idea gets a website. Digital storefronts bloom across the web like chrome flowers in an infinite garden.

🎵

Digital Sound

MP3 players with brushed-metal interfaces. Music goes digital. A thousand songs on a device that gleams.

Transparent Tech

See-through iMacs, translucent Game Boys, and crystal-clear gadgets. The future is meant to be looked through, not just at.

Cyber Cafes

Neon-lit rooms with rows of glowing monitors. The gateway to the world wide web, one dial-up connection at a time.

Holographic Interface

Shifting colors catch the light as you move. This iridescent border treatment reflects the era's fascination with holographic stickers, CD-ROM surfaces, and foil trading cards.

Digital Utopia

Information wants to be free. The network expands every second. We are building a world where geography dissolves and everyone is one click away from everything.

07

Alerts & Notifications

System notifications with frosted backgrounds and rounded corners. Each state uses a distinct color from the Y2K palette to signal its intent.

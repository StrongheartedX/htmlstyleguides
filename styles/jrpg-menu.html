IX

Design Principles

Core guidelines for creating authentic JRPG menu interfaces.

Ornate Borders

Multi-layer borders with beveled edges create the classic menu window look. Use corner decorations and golden accents for headers and important elements.

Gradient Depth

Blue-to-dark gradients give menus a sense of depth and dimensionality. The darker bottom edge grounds the interface while the lighter top draws the eye.

Text Shadows

Drop shadows on text ensure readability against gradient backgrounds. Cream and white text with subtle shadows feels both legible and stylistically authentic.

Animated Cursors

The bouncing arrow cursor is iconic to JRPGs. Use smooth animations to indicate selection and guide player attention through menu navigation.

Status at a Glance

HP and MP bars with color-coded warning states let players quickly assess their party condition. Red flashing for critical health creates urgency.

Nostalgic Typography

Fantasy-styled fonts for headers evoke the genre while clean body text maintains usability. Balance decorative flair with functional clarity.

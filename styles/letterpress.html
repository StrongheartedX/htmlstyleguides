IX

Design Principles

I

Impression

Every element should feel pressed into the page, creating depth through light and shadow that suggests tactile quality.

II

Restraint

Use color sparingly and deliberately. A limited palette creates sophistication; abundance creates noise.

III

Heritage

Honor typographic tradition. Classic serifs and generous spacing connect us to centuries of printing excellence.

IV

Materiality

Digital design should evoke physical craft. Paper texture, ink bleed, and embossing effects create warmth and authenticity.

V

Permanence

Design for longevity. Trends fade, but craftsmanship endures. Create work that will age gracefully.

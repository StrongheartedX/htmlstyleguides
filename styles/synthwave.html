Design Principles

01
Dramatic Warmth

Lead with the sunset palette. Every composition should feel like it is backlit by a horizon on fire -- warm oranges and pinks transitioning to cool violets and deep blues. The warmth is earnest, never ironic.

02
Chrome Precision

Metallic text and reflective surfaces convey the polished, high-performance feel of the aesthetic. Chrome is used for emphasis and hierarchy, catching light the way a sports car catches the eye.

03
Vanishing Point

Perspective and depth are essential. The grid road, the converging lines, the sense of speed and distance -- every layout should pull the eye toward a focal point on the horizon.

04
Cinematic Scale

Think movie poster, not UI mockup. Big type, bold gradients, generous whitespace. The synthwave aesthetic is about grandeur and atmosphere, not about cramming in content.

05
Restrained Glow

Neon effects are supporting actors, not the lead. Use glow sparingly to accent interactive elements and key focal points. Overuse dilutes the drama and strains the eye.

06
Forward Motion

The aesthetic is about movement -- driving, flying, racing toward something. Animations should be smooth and directional. Static compositions should still imply velocity and purpose.

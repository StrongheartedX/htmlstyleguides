09

Design Principles

The philosophy behind the neon-lit dystopia.

01

High-Tech, Low-Life

Advanced technology exists alongside urban decay. Interfaces should feel powerful yet gritty, sophisticated yet accessible to the street.

02

Neon Against Void

Vibrant colors cut through darkness like holographic signs in acid rain. Use color sparingly but boldly for maximum impact.

03

Angular Aggression

Sharp corners and clipped edges suggest danger and precision. Nothing is soft or rounded in the megacity.

04

Data is Everything

Information density matters. Show system status, metrics, and feedback. Users should feel connected to the machine.

05

Embrace Imperfection

Glitches, noise, and corruption add authenticity. Perfect systems dont exist in a world of hackers and corporate warfare.

06

Function Over Form

Every element serves a purpose. Decoration is minimal but meaningful. The interface is a tool, not art for arts sake.

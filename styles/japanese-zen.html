Wabi-Sabi - Design Principles

Design Principles

Seven aesthetic principles from Japanese Zen that guide this design system.

Kanso

Simplicity

Eliminate what is unnecessary. The absence of clutter creates clarity of purpose. Each element must earn its place.

Fukinsei

Asymmetry

Perfect symmetry is lifeless. Controlled irregularity creates movement, interest, and a sense of natural growth.

Shibui

Subtle Beauty

Beauty that is understated rather than bold. It reveals itself slowly, rewarding patient observation.

Shizen

Naturalness

Avoid the artificial and contrived. Design should feel organic, as if it emerged naturally rather than being forced.

Yugen

Subtle Profundity

Suggest rather than reveal. Leave room for imagination and interpretation. The unseen is as important as the seen.

Datsuzoku

Freedom

Break from convention when necessary. Rules serve the design, not the other way around. Find freedom within constraints.

Seijaku

Tranquility

Create moments of stillness and calm. In the quiet spaces between interactions, users find clarity and peace. The design breathes.

"In the midst of movement and chaos, keep stillness inside of you. True beauty is found not in perfection, but in the embrace of impermanence."

Zen Teaching

Ichi-go ichi-e

One moment, one meeting. This design system embraces the transient beauty of each interaction.

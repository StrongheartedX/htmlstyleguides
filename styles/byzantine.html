IX

Design Principles

Sacred Splendor

Every element should evoke the transcendent beauty of Byzantine churches. Gold dominates as the color of divine light, with jewel tones providing contrast and hierarchy.

Hieratic Formality

Maintain ceremonial dignity through symmetry, centered compositions, and formal typography. The design should command reverence and contemplation.

Tesserae Texture

Surfaces should suggest the handcrafted quality of mosaic work. Subtle grid patterns and shimmer effects evoke thousands of tiny tiles catching light.

Interlocking Geometry

Use circles, mandorlas, and Greek key patterns to create visual unity. These forms represent cosmic order and the interconnection of heaven and earth.

Imperial Authority

Purple and crimson convey power and martyrdom. Use these colors sparingly to denote importance and create visual hierarchy within the golden realm.

Luminous Depth

Create the illusion of inner light through gradients, glows, and shimmer animations. Backgrounds should feel like they emanate golden radiance.

Byzantine

A design system inspired by the sacred mosaics and imperial grandeur of the Eastern Roman Empire, where gold tesserae caught the light of heaven.

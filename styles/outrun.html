10

Design Principles

01
Sunset First

Every composition should feel backlit by a horizon on fire. Warm oranges and corals transition to cool violets and deep navy. The warmth is always earnest, never ironic.

02
Chrome Surfaces

Metallic gradients convey the polished, high-performance nature of the aesthetic. Chrome catches light the way a sports car catches the eye -- it demands attention without asking for it.

03
Vanishing Point

Perspective and depth are non-negotiable. The grid road, the converging palm rows, the sense of infinite distance. Every layout pulls the eye toward a focal point on the horizon.

04
Restrained Glow

Neon effects are supporting actors, not the lead. Use glow to accent interactive elements and key focal points. Overuse turns drama into noise. Less neon, more impact.

05
Forward Motion

The Outrun aesthetic is fundamentally about movement -- driving, accelerating, chasing the horizon. Animations should be smooth and directional. Even static compositions imply velocity.

06
VHS Warmth

Embrace analog imperfection. Scanlines, slight color bleed, warm noise. The aesthetic is nostalgic for a future that existed only on magnetic tape and in the amber glow of CRT screens.
